Microsoft Excel

This course taught us Microsoft Office Excel skills and how it is used in academic and business environments. We applied software features to the successful completion of business-related projects and scenarios.

    Some key takeaways I took from this course include:
  • Using formulas to aggregate data is one of the most basic functions of Excel, and to do so is relatively quick and easy.
  • Cell formatting and conditional formatting can be used to draw a users attention to certain cells and improve the visuals and readability of a worksheet.
  • Many charting tools are available in Excel to quickly visualize datasets.


Systems Analysis and Design

Systems Analysis and Design is a broad term for describing methodologies for developing high quality Information Systems which combine Information Technology, people, and data to support business requirements. This course focused heavily on the system development life cycle.

    Some key takeaways I took form this course include:
  • Planning is critical; all successful projects start with a strong plan.
  • Visual aids are incredibly valuable in both planning and development.
  • When designing databases, there are important rules and best practices to follow, including various levels of 'normal form.'

Business Analytics

This course taught us how to utilize common business software to analyze datasets present in typical business management situations, translate the analysis into business recommendations that will improve business performance, and effectively create and presente analysis recommendations to decision-makers.

    Some key takeaways I took from this course include:
  • Sparklines are like mini line graphs that are contained to one cell, and can be useful to visualize tends or compare multiple datasets side-by-side without taking much real-estate.
  • Pivot tables allow you to instantly summarize and explore large datasets without the use of formulas.
  • INDEX and MATCH combine to create a powerful and flexible lookup tool considered superior to VLOOKUP, allowing you to find values in any column in a group.

Project Management

This course taught us to apply the skills and tools necessary to design, implement, and evaluate formal projects. We demonstrated the application of the role of project management by developing a project proposal, using relevant software, working with project teams, sequencing tasks, charting progress, dealing with variations, budgets and resources, implementing a project, and assessing the outcome.

    Some key takeaways I took from this course include:
  • Being able to work with a team is critical to project management, and being able to lead but also compromise when necessary is a valuable skill.
  • Microsoft Project is a great tool for planning and tracking timelines and deliverables throughout a project.
  • Being able to communicate clearly and effectively is a must. If you're managing different people on different tasks they need to understand clearly what it is they are to be working on.
